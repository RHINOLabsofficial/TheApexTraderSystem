01

The Real Enemy

Most people who begin trading believe their greatest challenge is learning the right strategies. That is wrong. Your greatest adversary sits between your ears.

⚡ The Uncomfortable Truth

You can know the perfect strategy and still consistently lose money.

Research shows: traders who outperform their own strategies in backtests underperform those same strategies in real time. The difference: emotion. When real money is at stake, the brain fires the same distress signals as physical danger. The prefrontal cortex disengages under loss. Impulse takes over. Strategy is forgotten.

This module is not a motivational post. It is an operational system — clear rules, concrete tools and routines that ensure your mind does not work against your account.

Kernprinzip

A profitable trader is not someone who is always right. They are someone who consistently minimises wrong decisions — through systems, not willpower. Willpower is a finite resource. Systems are not.

02

Knowing & Controlling Emotions

You cannot control an emotion you do not recognise. Honest self-awareness is the first step — and understanding precisely what each emotion does to your trading decisions.

😡
Anger & Frustration
After a loss or missed opportunity. Leads to impulsive, oversized trades — "I will get it back right now."
Countermeasure

20-minute mandatory break after any loss exceeding 1R. Close the platform, step away, perform the breathing exercise.

😰
Fear & Panic
Leads to premature closing of winning trades, hesitation on valid setups, panic exits on normal market noise.
Countermeasure

Before the trade: define EXACTLY where stop and target are. Write it down. If stop is not triggered → no exit. The plan is the plan.

🤑
Gier
Appears after wins or a missed move. Leads to overtrading, oversized positions and poor entries.
Countermeasure

Define a daily trade limit and stick to it. After a successful day: stop early. Lock in gains, do not expand them.

😤
FOMO
A move has started, you missed it. Now you want to "get in" — usually just before the reversal.
Countermeasure

Mantra: "There is always the next trade." A missed opportunity is not a loss. A FOMO entry is.

🎰
Overconfidence
After a winning streak: "I have figured out the market." Risk is increased, rules ignored. The next major loss follows reliably.
Countermeasure

Never increase position size above the defined maximum — regardless of the winning streak. Statistics think, ego does not.

😶
Analysis Paralysis
After several losses: inability to trade even when valid setups appear. Endless waiting for "more confirmation."
Countermeasure

Mandatory paper trading after 3 consecutive losses. Rebuild confidence incrementally through demo successes.

Risk Management

Risk management is not optional — it is the only thing that keeps you in the game long-term. With a 35% win rate you are profitable if your average winner is 2× the size of your average loser. Mathematics beats instinct every time.

💀
Rule: Maximum 1–2% Risk per Trade
▼
At 1% risk per trade you need 100 consecutive total losses to empty the account. At 10%: just 10 consecutive losses.
  • Small risk = psychological freedom to think clearly
  • You make better decisions without existential pressure on each trade
  • 10% loss: only 11.1% gain needed to recover. 50% loss: 100% needed. The asymmetry is brutal.
🎯
Rule: Minimum 1:2 R:R Before Entry
▼
At 1:2 R:R you are profitable at a 34% win rate. You can lose 66% of your trades and still make money.
  • Calculate R:R ALWAYS before entry — never after
  • No clean 1:2 available → no trade
  • Stop must be ATR-based — no arbitrarily tight stop
📉
Rule: Define and Enforce a Daily Drawdown Limit
▼
Recommendation: 3–5% of account per day. Limit reached: trading day is over. No "one last attempt."
  • Bad days cluster — this is statistically normal, not personal failure
  • Continuing to trade on a bad day typically makes it worse
  • The limit trains discipline and prevents catastrophic losses
🔄
Rule: The Stop Loss is Sacred — Never Adjust Against Yourself
▼
Your stop is set — and it stays there. Always.
  • Moving the stop further away when threatened = removing the stop = destroying the strategy
  • Permitted: move stop to break-even once the position is profitable
  • Permitted: trailing stop in a profitable trade
  • Never permitted: moving the stop further away on a losing trade to delay the pain
04

The 10 Rules of a Profitable Trader

Rules are not restrictions — they are freedom. With clear rules, you need not make new decisions under stress. You follow the plan. That is professional trading.

01

I only trade defined setups

No trade without at least 3 confluence factors. "Looks good" is not a setup.

02

Stop, Target and Position Size BEFORE Entry

All three parameters are calculated and written down BEFORE the trade is opened.

03

Enforce the daily trade limit

Quality beats quantity. More trades = more emotional decisions = more errors.

04

No trading before/after high-impact news

30 minutes before and after NFP, CPI, FOMC: no new trade. News events can destroy any TA-based position within seconds.

05

After 3 consecutive losses: mandatory break

Three consecutive losses signal that either the market or your mindset is off. Both require distance.

06

No Revenge Trading — ever

No trade with the goal of "getting it back." Losses are a cost of doing business. Revenge trading is gambling.

07

Respect the daily drawdown limit

Limit reached: trading day is over. Close the laptop. Take a walk. No negotiating.

08

Let winners run, cut losers short

Closing winning trades early out of fear is at least as damaging as staying in losing trades too long.

09

Document every trade in the journal

Entry, exit, reason, emotion, screenshot. No exceptions. Without data there is no improvement.

10

Weekly review: honest analysis

Every Sunday: review the journal. Identify patterns in losses. Do not punish — analyse and improve.

06

Revenge & Over-Trading

These are the two most common account-destroyers. Not bad strategies. Not unlucky markets. Both have an identical cause: emotional loss of control.

⚡ Critical Insight

A single uncontrolled revenge trade can erase weeks of profitable trading.

You earn 2% all week. One revenge trade on Friday evening: -5%. The week ends at -3%. Psychologically it feels as though you never won — and you do not understand why you even showed up.

🔄
Revenge Trading — Recognition and Interruption
▼
Warning signs:
  • "I need to get this back" or "this market owes me a win"
  • Position size is larger than normal
  • The setup does not meet your usual criteria
  • Trade opened directly after a loss without a break
Interruption protocol:
  • 1. Recognise: "This is a revenge impulse"
  • 2. Close the trading platform
  • 3. Box breathing (4 rounds)
  • 4. Only then: re-read the journal entry for the last trade
  • 5. Decide soberly whether a new trade makes sense
📊
Over-Trading — The "always in the market" problem
▼
Stems from boredom, FOMO or the feeling of "not using time in front of the chart."
  • More trades ≠ more profit — often the opposite
  • Every additional trade pays spread/commission and increases emotional stress
  • Define a minimum waiting time between trades (e.g. 30 minutes)
  • Set the daily trade limit in writing and keep it visible
🧘
The 20-Minute Rule — your most important tool
▼
After EVERY loss exceeding 1R: 20-minute mandatory break. No exceptions.
  • Scientifically established: emotional reactions to financial losses last 15–20 minutes
  • During this time: no charts, no phone with broker app, ideally step outside
  • After 20 minutes: a sober decision about whether to continue
  • This single tool prevents 80% of all revenge trades

07

Mindset Self-Check

Be honest with yourself. These questions have no "right" or "wrong" — but the patterns in your answers will tell you where you currently stand and what is still holding you back.

MINDSET SELF-CHECK — 8 QUESTIONS

1. Your stop-loss was just triggered. Your first reaction is...
"This happens. I analyse why and move on."
"I open a new trade immediately — this time I will get it right."
"I cannot trade any more today — too shaken."
2. You see a strong move that you missed. You...
Note it and wait for the next valid setup.
Enter anyway — the move is still running, so you jump in.
Feel frustrated and nervously search for the next trade.
3. You have had 5 consecutive winning trades. What changes for you?
Nothing — you continue trading by your rules.
You increase position size — you are "on fire" right now.
You become more cautious — afraid of losing the gains.
4. Your account lost 4% this week. How do you react?
You analyse the losing trades and look for patterns to avoid.
You increase risk to recover the losses faster.
You stop for a week without understanding what went wrong.
5. You found no valid setup — the entire day was "boring." You...
Do nothing. No setup, no trade. You log the day as "No valid setup."
Eventually open a trade — "something will happen eventually."
6. Someone in a forum shows you their "1000% trade." Your reaction?
Indifference. One trade says nothing about a strategy.
You immediately want to risk more — "why am I not doing that?"
7. Your trade is up 1.5R. Suddenly a small pullback. You...
Let the trade run. Your target is 2R — the plan is the plan.
Close immediately — better to lock in 1R than risk giving it back.
8. What is your honest definition of a "good trading day"?
A day where I followed my rules 100% — whether I profited or not.
A day where I made money.
